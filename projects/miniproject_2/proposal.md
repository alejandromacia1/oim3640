
# My Project Proposal:

## What I’m building:
For this second project, I am building a Python program that extracts the dialogue of the novel Wuthering Heights and analyzes/compares how much each of the two protagonists, Heathcliff and Catherine, speak throughout the chapters of the book. My goal for this project is to quantify their dialogue dominance and monitor how it changes between chapters, ultimately developing a more data-driven comprehension of their roles. 


## Why I chose this:
One of my favorite activities is going to the movie theaters with my friends every Tuesday. A couple of weeks ago, they suggested watching Wuthering Heights, which stars Jacob Elordi and Margot Robbie. I really enjoyed the movie and its themes, which made me curious about the original story behind it. 

Later on, I found out that the movie was originally a film adaptation of the novel: Wuthering Heights, and when I first visited the site Project Gutenberg, one of the books featured in the first page was ironically Wuthering Heights. Since it was a recent movie I had seen (as well as one of the longest movies I have ever seen), I thought it would be interesting to analyze how the narrative balance between the two main characters, Heathcliff and Catherine, works. 

With this project, I wanted to see if I could use Python to compare the dialogue dominance between these two characters in the novel and analyze how their presence shifts throughout the whole story and the book, respectively.


## Core Features:
- One of the core features of the program is that it extracts the dialogue from the text and it assigns it to the characters being analyzed using a contextual speaker detection mechanism. 

- Includes a comparison between several metrics, including the total words spoken per character, their vocabulary usage, as well as the top words used by each of the characters. 

- Furthermore, the program analyzes the dialogue dominance of the characters across the chapters of the novel and how their presence changes as the book progresses. 


## What I don’t know yet:
Some of the complications I believe I will find as I build this program include: 
- Knowing when to accurately identify the correct speaker, particularly when the dialogue attribution is either indirect or ambiguous.

- How to deal with narrative structures that are more complicated (e.g: in situations where there are missing speaker markers or in situations where there are multiple speakers, or when nested dialogues occur).
